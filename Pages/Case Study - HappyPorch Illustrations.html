HappyPorch Illustrations

Timescale

Multiple sprints over approx. 3 years, spanning from 2021 to 2024 - potentially ongoing. Lenth of sprints varied based on urgency and other ongoing projects, but I usually had around 1 month of iteration and feedback cycles.

Brief

  • Based off of 4 existing brand illustrations, expand the library of available graphics for use on the HappyPorch website, various marketing materials and social media posts.
  • While maintaining cohesion, incorporate some more organic shapes and imagery.
  • Utilise the simple line drawing style, width and colour of the existing graphics.
  • Visually reference the intersection of technology (especially circular economy concepts) and natural imagery to highlight HappyPorch’s commitment to addressing environmental issues with their software solutions.

Problems/Constraints

Time - I had to take this project from basic wireframes - to a cohesive design within a day. Complex custom illustrations or highly experimental elements were out of the question, in favour of a solid font, colour palette and element styling.

Development time - The time available for initial implementation & client feedback was 1 week in total. I was mindful of the complexity/time-intensity of the design in terms of frontend dev work, and tried to think about the most straightforward styling changes that would have maximum impact.

Solution

I started by researching the project itself - on its website, CommunityCred defines itself as:
“a feasibility study exploring the potential for a mutual credit system to accelerate Scotland's transition to a circular economy and promote community wealth building. Our platform maps local trading networks, identifies opportunities for resource sharing, and supports the development of sustainable local supply chains.

"By connecting local businesses, social enterprises, charities, and community groups, we aim to strengthen local economies while promoting environmental sustainability and social inclusion.
CommunityCred is a collaborative project between University of Edinburgh researchers, circular economy organisations Ostreroand Lower Impact Living, and software engineering company Happy Porch.”

From this, I identified some key tonal characteristics to help direct my creative choices:

Community-focused: friendly, warm, accessible

For businesses, social enterprises, charities, community groups: trustworthy, clean, professional
