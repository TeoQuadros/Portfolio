the story

The idea to refresh the HappyPorch website had been a low-priority backburner task for a while. I was spending spare work time attempting to chart a new slightly new direction for the website's look and feel. With only vague guiding ideas of enhanced carbon efficiency and a wide layout, nothing really stuck.

Then I learned about the inspiration behind the company name - a poem called ‘The Sunlight on the Garden’ by Louis MacNeice. The final stanza goes as follows:


And not expecting pardon,
Hardened in heart anew,
But glad to have sat under
Thunder and rain with you,
And grateful too
For sunlight on the garden.

This so perfectly encapsulates the HappyPorch vibe, and I got really inspired to capture these ideas of warmth, camaraderie and optimism, paired with a healthy dose of realism - through good times and bad.

concept

I developed systems to make applying our new branding quick, easy and consistent. I created interactive Figma boards and Canva templates documenting layouts, colours and typographic hierarchies, suitable for the whole team.

I embarked on a self-directed learning project all about web accessibility standards, applying my newfound understanding of WCAG to our brand colours.

social media / marketing

When designing for socials, I keep in mind that the software industry is full of polished, corporate styling, and Barry is keen to set HappyPorch apart in this regard. I add playful touches like the doodle graphics and a conversational tone. While still incorporating technical imagery, I reflect the company’s commitment to people and the planet by letting the designs feel warm, approachable and human.
